# 【标题】第17期【作者】巴利圣典协会学报（JOURNAL OF THE PALI TEXT SOCIETY）【时间】1992【宗派】期刊

# 论文1. 探索《 Saddaniti》
作者：E.G. 卡尔斯（E.G. Kahrs）  
摘要：本文以阿迦瓦姆萨（Aggavamsa）所著巴利语语法巨著《Saddaniti》的“格关系”（karaka）章节为核心，系统探究这部12世纪缅甸语法著作的性质、来源与学术价值。研究梳理了《Saddaniti》与迦旃延（Kaccayana）、摩伽拉那（Moggallana）等前贤巴利语语法的传承关系，重点分析其对梵语语法传统（尤其是巴尼尼语法）的借鉴与改造。通过对施事、受事、工具、处所等格关系的定义、分类及示例考证，揭示该书既试图贴合巴利语经典语料，又受梵语语法框架束缚的矛盾特征。研究指出，《Saddaniti》虽被推崇为现存最全面的巴利语语法，但存在分类混乱、示例牵强等问题，其核心价值在于反映了12世纪缅甸的巴利语研究水平及南传佛教文献传承的语言规范诉求。  
关键词：《Saddaniti》；巴利语语法；格关系；阿迦瓦姆萨；梵语语法影响  
Title：Exploring the Saddaniti  
Author：E.G. Kahrs  
Abstract：This paper focuses on the "karaka" chapter of the Saddaniti, a monumental Pali grammar work by Aggavamsa, to systematically explore the nature, sources, and academic value of this 12th-century Burmese grammatical text. The study sorts out the inheritance relationship between the Saddaniti and previous Pali grammars by Kaccayana, Moggallana, and other predecessors, focusing on analyzing its absorption and transformation of the Sanskrit grammatical tradition (especially Paninian grammar). Through the textual research on the definition, classification, and examples of karaka categories such as agent, object, instrument, and locus, it reveals the contradictory characteristics of the book: it attempts to conform to Pali canonical corpus while being constrained by the Sanskrit grammatical framework. The study points out that although the Saddaniti is regarded as the most comprehensive extant Pali grammar, it has problems such as chaotic classification and forced examples. Its core value lies in reflecting the level of Pali research in 12th-century Burma and the linguistic norm demands of the transmission of Theravada Buddhist literature.  
Keywords：Saddaniti；Pali grammar；karaka；Aggavamsa；Sanskrit grammatical influence

# 论文2. 刹帝利为何被称为刹帝利？——《阿含经·起世因本经》再探
作者：理查德·冈布里奇（Richard Gombrich）  
摘要：本文重新审视《阿含经·起世因本经》（Aggañña Sutta）中关于社会起源的神话叙事，聚焦“刹帝利”（khattiya）一词的词源阐释。经文中将“刹帝利”溯源至“khettanam pati”（田主），这与该神话中统治者因维护秩序而获稻米份额的情节并不契合。研究指出，这一词源解释实则是对婆罗门教王室 consecration仪式中梵语咒语“ksatranam ksatrapatir asi”（你是权力之主）的戏仿，将“ksatra”（权力）扭曲为“ksetra”（田地）。结合经文对其他种姓名称的词源调侃及与《百道梵书》等吠陀文献的关联，证实该神话具有讽刺婆罗门教社会起源论的意图，为佛教社会伦理思想提供了文本依据。  
关键词：刹帝利；《起世因本经》；词源学；婆罗门教；佛教神话  
Title：Why is a Khattiya called a Khattiya? The Aggañña Sutta Revisited  
Author：Richard Gombrich  
Abstract：This paper re-examines the mythological narrative of social origin in the Aggañña Sutta, focusing on the etymological explanation of the term "khattiya" (Kshatriya). The sutta traces "khattiya" to "khettanam pati" (lord of the fields), which is inconsistent with the plot in the myth that rulers receive a share of rice for maintaining order. The study points out that this etymological interpretation is actually a parody of the Sanskrit mantra "ksatranam ksatrapatir asi" (Thou art the power-lord of the powers) in the Brahminical royal consecration ceremony, distorting "ksatra" (power) into "ksetra" (fields). Combined with the sutta's satirical etymologies of other varna names and its connection with Vedic texts such as the Satapatha Brahmana, it is confirmed that the myth intends to satirize the Brahminical theory of social origin, providing textual evidence for Buddhist social ethics.  
Keywords：Khattiya；Aggañña Sutta；etymology；Brahmanism；Buddhist myth

# 论文3. 巴利语词典学研究（十一）——两个巴利语词汇的词源考
作者：K.R. 诺曼（K.R. Norman）  
摘要：本文针对《巴利语-英语词典》（PED）中未收录或释义、词源有误的两个巴利语词汇展开考证。其一为“sama”，词典误将“agginisama”中的“sama”释为“柴堆”，实则应为“类似”之义；而“sama”表“年”的义项，可通过《法句经注》（Dhp-a）及《大史注》（Mhv-t）的注释佐证，其对应梵语“samvacchara”，常见于纪年语境。其二为数字“dvatthi”（62），词典仅列形式未释来源，该词实为中印度雅利安语方言形式，因元音间-s-脱落形成，其注释者已明确其非标准巴利语特征，补充了巴利语数字方言变体的研究空白。  
关键词：巴利语；词典学；词源考证；sama；dvatthi  
Title：Pali Lexicographical Studies X: Two Pali Etymologies  
Author：K.R. Norman  
Abstract：This paper conducts textual research on two Pali words that are either unincluded or have incorrect meanings or etymologies in the Pali-English Dictionary (PED). The first is "sama": the dictionary mistakenly interprets "sama" in "agginisama" as "pyre", but it should actually mean "like"; the meaning of "sama" as "year" can be confirmed by annotations in the Dhammapada-atthakatha (Dhp-a) and the Mahavamsa-tika (Mhv-t), corresponding to Sanskrit "samvacchara" and commonly used in chronological contexts. The second is the numeral "dvatthi" (62): the dictionary only lists the form without explaining its origin. This word is actually a Middle Indo-Aryan dialectal form, formed by the elision of intervocalic -s-, and its commentator has clearly identified its non-standard Pali characteristics, supplementing the research gap of dialectal variants of Pali numerals.  
Keywords：Pali；lexicography；etymological research；sama；dvatthi
